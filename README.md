# Jewellery Sales Dashboard (Excel)

## Problem Statement

Analyze jewellery store sales performance to identify top-performing products, revenue trends, and city-wise sales distribution.

![jewellery_dashboard_preview](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6a53a343-c610-40c9-953f-366fb2e9f268)


## Tools Used

• Microsoft Excel
• Pivot Tables
• Pivot Charts
• Slicers

## Dashboard Features

• KPI metrics (Total Sales, Cost, Profit, Profit Margin)
• Monthly sales trend analysis
• Sales distribution by product category
• Top-performing products by revenue
• Interactive filters for dynamic data exploration

## Key Insights

• Necklaces generated the highest revenue among all product categories.
• Sales increased significantly during festive periods.
• Ahmedabad and Pune contributed the largest share of total sales.

# HR Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

## Problem Statement

Analyze employee attrition within an organization to understand **which factors contribute to employees leaving the company** and identify patterns that can help improve workforce retention.

<img width="1352" height="761" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b403d009-cb58-43f6-a607-fa96442d99b4" />


## Tools Used

• Power BI
• Data Modeling
• DAX Measures
• Interactive Filters (Slicers)

## Dashboard Features

• HR KPI metrics (Total Employees, Employees Left, Attrition Rate, Average Salary)
• Attrition analysis by Department
• Employee attrition by Age Group
• Attrition distribution across Job Roles
• Impact of Overtime on employee turnover
• Attrition analysis by Education Field
• Interactive filters for Gender, Department, and Marital Status

## Key Insights

• Research & Development department experienced the highest attrition.
• Employees aged **26–35** had the highest turnover.
• Job roles such as **Laboratory Technician and Sales Executive** showed the highest attrition.
• Employees working **overtime were more likely to leave the company**.
• Certain education backgrounds contribute more to employee turnover.
